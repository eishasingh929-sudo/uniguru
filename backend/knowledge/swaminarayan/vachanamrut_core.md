## Content

The Vachanamrut (Vachana = Word, Amrut = Nectar) is the principal scripture of the Swaminarayan Sampradaya. It is a compilation of 273 discourses delivered by Bhagwan Swaminarayan between 1819–1829 CE, transcribed by his disciples and compiled into a canonical text.

**Structure:**
The 273 discourses are organized by location:
- Gadhada (I, II, III) — 76 + 67 + 39 discourses
- Sarangpur — 18 discourses
- Kariyani — 12 discourses
- Loya — 18 discourses
- Panchala — 7 discourses
- Vartal — 20 discourses
- Amdavad — 3 discourses
- Jetalpur — 4 discourses
- Bhugol-Khagol (extra additions)

**Key Philosophical Doctrines:**
1. **Panch Vartman** — Five moral vows: abstinence from meat, intoxicants, theft, adultery, and impurity
2. **Upasana** — The understanding that Swaminarayan is Purushottam (Supreme Being), par-kar (possessing divine form), sarvopari (supreme above all devis/devatas), and pragat (ever-present)
3. **The Four Entities** — Para-brahma (Supreme God), Akshara-brahma (eternal abode), maya (illusion), and jivas (individual souls)

**Gadhada I-1 (The First Discourse):**
Swaminarayan asks: "What is the characteristic of an 'ekantik-bhakta'?"
Answer: One who possesses the four pillars of dharma, jnan, vairagya, and bhakti is an ekantik-bhakta (ultimate devotee).
