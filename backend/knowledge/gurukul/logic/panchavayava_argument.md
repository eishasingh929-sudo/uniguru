The classical Nyaya presentation of inference is often described through five parts:
1. Pratijna: the thesis or proposition.
2. Hetu: the reason offered in support of the thesis.
3. Udaharana: the example that demonstrates the rule.
4. Upanaya: the application of that rule to the current case.
5. Nigamana: the conclusion drawn from the reasoning.

This structure helps students distinguish a supported inference from an unsupported claim.
